# Ethical Use Policy

SPIDER is intended for lawful, authorized security assessment, education, and lab
research. It must not be used to access, scan, disrupt, exploit, or retain data
from systems without explicit permission.

## Acceptable Use

- Testing systems you own or administer.
- Testing systems covered by written authorization, such as a rules of
  engagement document, contract, or bug bounty policy.
- Running examples against the local lab or intentionally vulnerable training
  environments.
- Developing defensive research, detection logic, and safety controls.

## Prohibited Use

- Unauthorized reconnaissance, exploitation, credential access, persistence,
  lateral movement, exfiltration, or denial-of-service activity.
- Submitting real target data, secrets, PII, customer data, or private engagement
  artifacts to this repository.
- Removing or weakening scope checks, sandbox execution, HITL gates, or audit
  controls.
- Contributing payloads or workflows whose primary purpose is evasion,
  unauthorized access, persistence, or harm.

## Maintainer Review

Maintainers may reject or remove contributions, issues, discussions, or examples
that create unacceptable risk or conflict with this policy, even if the code is
technically functional.
